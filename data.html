Historical Education Quality Database

With financial support from the Stanford Center for International Development, the Stanford Institute for Innovation in Developing Economies, and The Europe Center at Stanford’s Freeman Spogli Institute, I am leading an effort to construct a panel dataset containing country-level historical measures of education quality and of the content of education policies relevant for how much learning takes place in schools (e.g., teacher training and recruitment policies, and policies regulating the primary school curriculum). In the initial stages, the dataset will span Latin American and European countries from 1870 to the present.

The dataset will be made available to the public upon its completion.

If you would like to contribute your expert knowledge of a country to this initiative, please contact me via email: paglayan@stanford.edu.

Endogenous Institutions: Evidence from Public Sector Collective Bargaining Laws in the Unites States

For this project, I digitized historical data to construct a panel with annual information on student-teacher ratios, average teacher salaries, and per-pupil education expenditures, for all states in the US, from 1959 to 1990. The dataset also contains sporadic information on the same variables at earlier points in the twentieth century, as well as information about the year in which each state introduced a law requiring districts to engage in collective bargaining with public school teachers.

The dataset will be made available here soon.
